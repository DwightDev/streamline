FAQ

Order management questions, answered.

What does Streamline include for bakery order management?

Streamline helps bakeries manage standing orders, one-off orders, online ordering, customer accounts, catalogues, pricing, order changes, invoicing, and customer communication in one connected system. It is designed to keep sales, customer service, production, delivery, and finance working from the same order data.

Can customers place bakery orders online with Streamline?

Yes. Streamline supports web and mobile ordering for wholesale customers, including standing orders, shopping carts for one-off orders and amendments, easy reordering, and saved templates. Order deadlines and product lead times can also be enforced so teams receive cleaner order data before production begins.

Can Streamline handle customer-specific pricing and item catalogues?

Yes. Streamline supports unlimited price lists, customer-specific pricing, item visibility by customer or delivery address, and templates for managing access at scale. It also supports seasonal item rules and date-based availability, helping bakeries control what each customer can order and when.

Can Streamline capture orders without manual re-entry?

Yes. In addition to web and mobile ordering, Streamline can capture orders from inbox formats using Streamline CE. It can listen for orders sent as PDF, HTML, Excel, or CSV and create them in the system, reducing manual order entry and helping teams work faster.

How does order management connect to invoicing and customer accounts?

Orders in Streamline connect directly to invoicing and customer account workflows. The system includes an invoice and credit note library, live customer statements, payment collection tools, and account-level settings such as contacts, addresses, delivery charges, and billing preferences. This helps keep order intake and finance aligned.
