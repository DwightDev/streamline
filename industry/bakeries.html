Run bakery operations from one connected ERP workflow.

FAQ

Bakery ERP software questions, answered.

What is bakery ERP software?

Bakery ERP software connects the operational workflows a bakery uses to run the business, including customer ordering, standing orders, production planning, recipes, inventory, lot tracking, delivery, invoicing, payments, accounting exports, and reporting.

How does Streamline help wholesale bakeries?

Streamline helps wholesale bakeries manage recurring customer orders, cart orders, price lists, customer catalogues, recipe production, ingredient usage, inventory tracking, route delivery, invoices, payments, and reporting from one connected system.

Can Streamline connect bakery orders to production planning?

Yes. Customer order demand can flow into production planning, recipe totals, recipe cards, ingredient demand, inventory updates, and production dates, helping teams plan what needs to be made based on actual orders and available stock.

Does Streamline support bakery inventory and traceability?

Yes. Streamline supports goods-in, supplier records, storage locations, stock counts, stock adjustments, ingredients, lot details, use-by dates, FEFO stock rotation, production usage, finished goods, delivery records, and recall workflows.

Can Streamline handle bakery delivery routes?

Yes. Streamline includes distribution workflows such as Route Manager, Pick & Pack, delivery notes, packing slips, delivery charges, driver reports, route optimization integrations, and delivery asset tracking.

Does Streamline integrate with QuickBooks?

Yes. Streamline connects with QuickBooks Online through the QuickBooks Dashboard and supports workflows for pairing, exporting, importing, invoices, credit notes, customers, items, vendors, and payments.

Is Streamline only for bakeries?

No. Bakery is a proven Streamline strength, but the platform is built as flexible ERP software for order-driven production and distribution businesses. That means it can also support food producers, commercial kitchens, prepared foods teams, specialty manufacturers, and route-based distributors with similar workflow needs.
