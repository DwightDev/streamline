Their story

From pioneering sourdough to managing a growing bakery operation.

Wild Wheat began in 1999, when Andrew Fearnside set out to bring nutritionally superior sourdough bread to New Zealand. What started as a small East Tamaki bakery grew steadily into a much larger operation with six shops, a sizeable wholesale trade, online sales, and a team of around 130 staff.

As Wild Wheat grew, so did the complexity behind the scenes. Production, recipes, ordering, reporting, delivery, purchasing, and customer information all needed to be managed with more structure and visibility.

The challenge was not replacing the craft of baking. It was organising the operational knowledge behind the bakery so the whole team could work from the same accurate information.

For years, much of that information lived in Andrew’s head or inside an ageing computer programme that needed constant tweaking. As production volumes increased, Wild Wheat needed a system that could turn that knowledge into clear reports, accessible workflows, and repeatable processes.

Turning 20 years of bakery knowledge into a connected system.

Working with Mountain Stream, Wild Wheat moved toward a cloud-based bakery operating system that could support the way the business actually worked. The onboarding process was not a quick copy-and-paste exercise. It involved understanding Wild Wheat’s production shifts, day and night teams, pastry operations, delivery drivers, recipes, reporting needs, and the many small details that keep a bakery running.

Andrew described the onboarding process as a major part of the success. Mountain Stream took the time to understand how Wild Wheat worked, then helped turn that knowledge into a system staff could use across the business.

"Tim has been fantastic in the on-boarding process... he has been amazing." — Andrew Fearnside, Wild Wheat

Clearer production, easier recipes, and better visibility.

With Streamline, Wild Wheat gained a system that connects online ordering, internal order entry, production reports, recipes, financial information, delivery workflows, and retrospective reporting.

The impact was felt across the business. Production staff could work from clear reports. Managers could check orders from the Mountain Stream app. Recipes could be scaled and printed accurately. Trends could be reviewed over several weeks to support production planning, staffing, and purchasing decisions.

For a seven-day bakery producing 40–50 product lines, that visibility matters. Instead of relying on memory, manual notes, or last-minute calculations, the team could work from a shared system built around real bakery operations.

A system built around how bakers actually work.

One of the biggest wins for Wild Wheat was recipe handling. Tasks that once required manual calculations could now be printed directly from the system with the correct ingredient ratios. Andrew described this as a game-changer for his team.

Streamline also helped Wild Wheat create bespoke reports, including delivery run reports for subcontracted drivers and operational reports tailored to the bakery’s production needs.

As Wild Wheat continues to grow, Mountain Stream gives the team the structure, reporting, and operational control to scale without going back to manual systems.
